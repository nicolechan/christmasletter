Cuba
July

Here we are learning how to dance salsa in Trinidad, Cuba. Fortunately, we were able to travel to Cuba on an individual tour before you-know-who (the president) closed the doors again so only large sanctioned groups could visit the island. We designed our own itinerary (wijincuba.com) and got around with a guide and driver. Our accommodations were almost exclusively ​"​casa​s​ particular​es", which is their equivalent of Airbnb. We had few expectations about Cuba since we knew little about it. Our trip was a lesson in the colonial history of this tobacco and sugar producing Caribbean island which was Spain's front door to conquering Latin America. We also learned the reasons for its geopolitical significance post-independence. The country is definitely poor, but the culture is rich. The people we saw in the cities and countries were friendly and seemed relatively happy despite their lack of material wealth.
Morocco
September

Tom and Patty had a great time exploring Morocco with friends. We loved venturing into the maze of stores in the souks (markets) with their exotic spices, homeopathic body treatments and artisan shoes, and peeking into doorways to see everyday life. We enjoyed understanding more about the rich Islamic and Berber cultures, visiting handicraft studios, sightseeing in Fes, Marrakech and Casablanca, savoring Morocco's delicious stews cooked in tagines (clay pots), and trekking the High Atlas mountains through remote villages.
